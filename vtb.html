Margaret Fabrizio: A Retrospective


95 Years of Curiosity, From Carnegie Hall to the Siddi Forest.

Margaret Fabrizio does not live in the past; she creates the future. At 95, Margaret is a San Francisco icon whose creative output is as prolific as it is unpredictable.

This pop-up retrospective will be a living 'collage' of a life dedicated to the 'what if?'—inviting you into a world where 17th-century Baroque music meets 21st-century YouTube performance.

Margaret's quilts are a masterclass in improvisation. Her practice was transformed in 2011 after a journey to India to study with the Siddi women. Learning the Kawandi technique—a method of hand-stitching from the outside-in—Margaret embraced a 'slow-stitch' philosophy.

Margaret speaking about wanting to show her quilts in public in 2013.

Long before she picked up a needle, Margaret was a pioneer of the harpsichord. As the first artist to perform Bach's Art of Fugue on the harpsichord in America, her musical career spanned from Carnegie Hall to the experimental energy of 1970s San Francisco.

Margaret has created over 80 'Portrait Masks', which gained global recognition when featured on the cover of the Grateful Dead's 1987 album, In the Dark. For Margaret, a mask is not a way to hide—it is a way to reveal.

In collaboration with master bookmaker Howard Munson, Margaret creates "architectures of memory" — handmade art books blending intricate collage with personal narrative. These tactile volumes represent a decades-long partnership and a shared devotion to craftsmanship, offering an intimate, detailed window into the finer textures of Margaret's vast imagination.

An 'early YouTuber' in every sense, Margaret has been documenting her creative experiments digitally since 2004. Her films don't just document her art. They are her art.
